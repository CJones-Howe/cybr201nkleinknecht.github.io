Quick History About Dogs!

The domestication of dogs is believed to have occurred between 20,000 and 40,000 years ago, making them one of the first animals to be domesticated by humans.

    The prevailing theory is that wolves, attracted to human campsites due to food scraps, gradually began to coexist with early humans. Over time, a mutually beneficial relationship formed.

    Humans started selectively breeding them for specific traits, like size, coat color, and behavior. This led to the many breeds of dogs we have today.

    Domestication helped dogs develop strong bonds with humans, making them valuable companions, workers, and protectors.

Dogs are loyal, friendly animals that come in all shapes and sizes. Whether you're a fan of energetic retrievers or calm bulldogs, there's a breed for everyone!
