Dog Breed Classifications!

Dog breeds are generally classified by their purpose or function. The American Kennel Club (AKC) organizes dog breeds into several groups based on their original characteristics:

    Sporting Group - These dogs were originally bred for hunting or retrieving.
    Examples: Labrador Retriever, Golden Retriever, Spaniels

    Hound Group - Hounds were bred for hunting by scent or sight.
    Examples: Beagle, Dachshund, Greyhound

    Working Group - These breeds were developed for specific jobs like guarding, pulling sleds, or water rescue.
    Examples: Siberian Husky, Rottweiler, Saint Bernard

    Terrier Group - Originally bred for hunting vermin, terriers are energetic and independent.
    Examples: Jack Russell Terrier, Bull Terrier, Scottish Terrier

    Toy Group - Small breeds often kept as companions.
    Examples: Chihuahua, Pomeranian, Shih Tzu

    Non-Sporting Group - This diverse group includes breeds with various origins.
    Examples: Bulldog, Poodle, Dalmatian

    Herding Group - These dogs were bred to herd livestock, and they are highly intelligent and energetic.
    Examples: Border Collie, German Shepherd, Collie

    Miscellaneous Group - This includes breeds that are in the process of being recognized by the AKC.
    Examples: Belgian Laekenois, Dogo Argentino
